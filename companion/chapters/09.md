# Chapter 9: Design Systems Turn Taste into Shared Infrastructure

## Product question

> Is this recurring interface decision best reused, extended, or created—and what contract makes that choice maintainable?

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Audit one recurring screen or interaction rather than starting with a component library.
2. Record inconsistent content, anatomy, tokens, variants, states, and behavior.
3. Check responsive, accessibility, loading, empty, error, success, and permission states.
4. Decide whether to reuse, extend, create, or deliberately leave local.
5. Define ownership, design-to-code parity, tests, rollout, and maintenance.

## Take it to your product

Produce one prioritized audit finding and one component contract. The goal is a shared decision the team can govern, not a bigger design system by default.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide, the Design Audit, and Component Contract. Ask me for one recurring interface decision and its current evidence. Help me compare reuse, extension, creation, and local treatment. Require state coverage, accessibility, responsive behavior, tokens, ownership, tests, and migration impact. Do not infer usability from visual consistency alone.

## Evidence boundary

An audit and component contract do not prove user value, accessibility conformance, adoption, or return on infrastructure investment. Those require design review, implementation inspection, testing, usage evidence, and governance.
