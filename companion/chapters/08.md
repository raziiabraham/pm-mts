## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Choose one consequential handoff rather than mapping an entire product.
2. Name the actors, object, starting state, intended end state, and ownership transfer.
3. Draw the happy path using observable state changes.
4. Break it with duplicates, invalid permissions, timeouts, missing data, provider failure, and recovery.
5. Convert the flow into acceptance criteria, event evidence, operational visibility, and unresolved decisions.

## Take it to your product

Complete one Flow-to-Acceptance Record and review the negative paths with the people who operate, design, build, and support the workflow.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide and the Flow-to-Acceptance Record. Interview me about one handoff. Keep actors, object states, events, and responsibilities explicit. After the happy path, challenge it with duplicate, timeout, permission, provider, and recovery cases. Produce acceptance criteria only from states we have agreed, and mark unresolved ownership questions.

## Evidence boundary

A flow makes assumptions inspectable; it does not prove the documented process matches production, that every exception is known, or that the proposed behavior is usable. Verify important paths with users, operators, system evidence, and specialists.
