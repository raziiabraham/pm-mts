## Five-minute walkthrough

Begin with reflective listening. Listen for the stakeholder's language and workarounds; clarify ambiguous terms; reflect the meaning and constraint back; challenge the provisional model with a counterexample; then summarize and reconstruct the trigger, states, evidence, and unresolved decisions. Ask the stakeholder to correct that reconstruction before drawing.

Then use the seven steps for flowcharting:

1. Name the question and keep one journey or operational outcome in scope.
2. Define the trigger and every meaningful terminal outcome.
3. Identify actors, systems, and ownership lanes.
4. Draft the happy path with concise verb + object actions.
5. Add decision questions, labelled branches, and system responses.
6. Add negative paths, recovery, ownership, and evidence.
7. Walk normal and difficult cases with the actors, repair gaps, and derive delivery language.

Use rounded endpoints for starts and finishes, rectangles for actions, diamonds for decisions, arrows for direction, and swimlanes for ownership. The notation should help another person follow the flow without learning a private visual language.

## Take it to your product

Complete the reflective-listening section of one Flow-to-Acceptance Record before drawing. Then review the flow and its negative paths with the people who operate, design, build, and support the workflow.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide and the Flow-to-Acceptance Record. Interview me about one handoff using reflective listening: listen, clarify ambiguous terms, reflect my meaning back, challenge the model with a counterexample, and summarize your reconstruction for correction. Do not draw yet. After I approve the reconstruction, guide me through the seven flowcharting steps. Keep actors, object states, events, and responsibilities explicit. Produce acceptance criteria only from states we have agreed, and mark unresolved ownership questions.

## Evidence boundary

A flow makes assumptions inspectable; it does not prove the documented process matches production, that every exception is known, or that the proposed behavior is usable. Verify important paths with users, operators, system evidence, and specialists.
