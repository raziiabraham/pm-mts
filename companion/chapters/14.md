## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Preserve the complete input, evidence, output, version, and user consequence.
2. Identify the earliest plausible failing layer and competing causes.
3. Choose the smallest credible change and name its regression risk.
4. Replay preserved, normal, boundary, and held-out cases.
5. Decide rollout, sampling, monitoring, fallback, and reversion evidence.

## Take it to your product

Run one governed calibration review. Change one layer at a time where possible and keep the versioned capability, evaluator, cost, latency, and exposure conditions connected to the decision.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Reconstruct the preserved failure and version chain directly in chat before suggesting a change. Run the safe record check yourself and explain its narrow scope. Offer competing causes, identify the smallest bounded intervention, and interview me through regression and production sampling. Do not reduce calibration to prompt tweaking.

## Agent-executed evidence

The agent runs this safe local integrity check and distinguishes it from production proof:

```sh
python3 companion/calibration/validate_record.py
```

## Evidence boundary

The validator proves that the supplied narrative stays connected to its constructed case and outputs. It does not prove the proposed change is reliable, economical, safe, or effective in production.
