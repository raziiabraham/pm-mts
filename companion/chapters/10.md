# Chapter 10: Product Psychology Explains Why Users Move or Stop

## Product question

> Which competing explanation best accounts for the target behavior, and what evidence could distinguish it?

## What the agent brings into chat

1. [Fogg and COM-B diagnosis figure](../figures/png/figure_10_1.png)
2. [Behavior Intervention Card](../templates/behavior_intervention_card.md)

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Define one observable target behavior, actor, and context.
2. Build an evidence ladder from observed facts to interpretations and unknowns.
3. Diagnose the behavior through both Fogg and COM-B rather than selecting one convenient story.
4. Write at least two competing hypotheses and interventions.
5. Define evidence, guardrails, reversal conditions, and a low-traffic plan.

## Take it to your product

Complete one Behavior Intervention Card and identify the smallest test that could distinguish competing explanations without manipulating users or overclaiming causality.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide and the Behavior Intervention Card. Ask me for observable evidence before naming a behavioral framework. Diagnose the target behavior through Fogg and COM-B, generate at least two competing explanations, and challenge each with disconfirming evidence. Help me design an ethical, reversible test and label every inference.

## Evidence boundary

Behavioral frameworks are diagnostic lenses, not literal maps of cognition or causal proof. A plausible explanation can still be wrong; verify it with appropriate qualitative, behavioral, experimental, and ethical evidence.
