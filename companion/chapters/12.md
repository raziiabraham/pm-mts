> Which solution shape best fits the problem, evidence, risk, cost, latency, control, and organisational readiness?

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Write the problem and decision without naming a technology.
2. Include no-build, manual workflow, and assisted-confirmation options.
3. Compare deterministic software, predictive ML, generative AI, hybrid, and buy options where relevant.
4. Evaluate evidence needs, quality, cost, latency, privacy, fallback, operations, and human authority.
5. Record the decision, rejected alternatives, assumptions, and trigger for revisiting it.

Use Figure 12.1 for the concrete Heatseeker solution shape. Use Figure 12.2 to inspect the four contracts that generalise beyond that case: what context is eligible, what the model may propose, who may authorise action, and what evidence earns the next change.

## Take it to your product

Complete one Solution-Fit Decision Record before writing a prompt or choosing a model. Review the responsibility boundary with engineering, data, design, security, legal, and operations as appropriate.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide and the AI Solution-Fit Decision Record. Keep the problem technology-neutral. Help me compare no-build, workflow, rules, predictive ML, generative AI, hybrid, and external-provider options. Challenge my preferred option on evidence, quality, cost, latency, privacy, fallback, operations, and human control. Do not select AI merely because generation is possible.

## Evidence boundary

A scored option table structures judgment; it is not empirical proof that the winner will work. Validate uncertain assumptions with prototypes, technical investigation, users, risk owners, and cost or latency measurements.
