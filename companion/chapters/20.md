## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Pressure-test the user, problem, evidence, scope, behavior, and exclusions before code.
2. Tie the implementation plan to actual repository patterns, checks, environments, and reviewers.
3. Verify product behavior and the engineering bar, including unhappy, permission, data, event, and operational paths.
4. Separate merge, deployment, exposure, rollout, and rollback.
5. Define measurement, GTM, boundary memo, ownership, and the next learning decision.

## Take it to your product

Carry one bounded improvement through the complete delivery slice. Another person should be able to inspect why it existed, what changed, how it was checked, who reviewed it, how it reached users, and what evidence came next.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide and the three linked records. Help me carry one bounded change from evidence to delivery. Do not equate generated code, passing checks, merge, deployment, and user exposure. Require product QA, technical checks, human review, rollout, rollback, measurement, GTM, and a boundary memo. Mark every claim that needs evidence from the real system.

## Evidence boundary

Passing automated checks does not prove the right product was built. Deployment does not prove exposure; exposure does not prove value; measurement does not automatically prove cause. Delivery remains a human-accountable chain of evidence and decisions.
