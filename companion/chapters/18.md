# Chapter 18: Discovery Becomes Evidence Operations

## Product question

> Which claims support this product decision, where did they come from, how strong are they, and what evidence should come next?

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Preserve the original user or business signal and its source.
2. Classify verified facts, likely interpretations, contradictions, unknowns, and next questions.
3. Link every consequential claim to evidence, confidence, decision impact, and successor artifact.
4. Prioritize an evidence plan around the riskiest assumptions rather than collecting more material indiscriminately.
5. End with a decision, rejected alternatives, uncertainty, and the next test.

## Take it to your product

Create an assumption and evidence map for one bounded product bet. Keep source links and artifact lineage intact as the claim moves into prototype, specification, implementation, and measurement.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide, the Assumption and Evidence Map, and Evidence Card. Help me classify each statement as verified fact, interpretation, contradiction, unknown, or question. Require a source for every factual claim and do not convert frequency into importance or correlation into cause. End with a prioritized evidence plan and decision boundary.

## Evidence boundary

An evidence map improves provenance and exposes uncertainty; it does not eliminate sampling bias, missing stakeholders, bad measurement, or motivated interpretation. Important claims still require appropriate research, data, domain, legal, and ethical review.
