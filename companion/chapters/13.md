## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Define the user decision and prohibited behavior before scoring outputs.
2. Inspect one held-out case, candidate set, rubric, and both version outputs.
3. Predict pass, fail, or abstain before reading the expected result.
4. Separate hard checks, semantic judgment, severity, repeatability, and reviewer roles.
5. Decide from release gates and critical cases—not an average alone.

## Take it to your product

Create a scorecard with representative, ambiguous, missing, adversarial, and no-answer cases; anchored rubric levels; evaluator ownership; abstention; cost and latency; and release-blocking failures.

## Evidence boundary

The supplied five-case gate is a constructed teaching example. Passing it does not prove generalisation, production safety, evaluator validity, fairness, or user value beyond the bounded cases and criteria.
