# Chapter 17: The Repo Is a Product Surface

## Product question

> What does the repository prove about this behavior, what does it contradict, and what product truth still requires people or runtime evidence?

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. State the user-visible behavior and run or observe the product where possible.
2. Read repository navigation and instruction files before searching broadly.
3. Map entry point, UI, data, events, flags, tests, delivery, and recent precedents.
4. Separate verified implementation facts from interpretations and human context.
5. Record one contradiction, one unresolved product decision, and the next safe action.

## Take it to your product

Build a repository orientation map before prescribing a change. Ask engineering which paths are current, safe to copy, legacy, generated, or owned elsewhere.

## Evidence boundary

Repository evidence describes implementation, not complete product intent, user behavior, operational history, or policy. A passing check confirms the cited mismatch at the inspected commit; it does not decide which policy is correct.
