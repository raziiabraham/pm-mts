## Broader SQL progression

Chapter 5 contains eight executable examples, not only the rentals-per-copy investigation. Read them in this order:

1. `SELECT`, `WHERE`, `ORDER BY`, and `LIMIT` for PG-rated films.
2. `COUNT(*)` and `GROUP BY` for catalog composition by rating.
3. `JOIN` across customer, rental, inventory, and film records.
4. A common table expression that normalizes rentals by inventory copies.
5. `SUM`, a half-open date range, and store-level revenue.
6. `COUNT(DISTINCT ...)` for inventory on open rental at a historical cutoff.
7. A common table expression plus subquery for above-average customer spend.
8. `CASE` for explicit analytical categories.

The complete runnable file is [chapter_5_examples.sql](../sql/sakila/queries/chapter_5_examples.sql). Each pattern answers a different kind of product question and carries a different interpretation risk.

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. State the product decision before reading the query.
2. Identify the output grain: one film per row.
3. Trace the `film → inventory → rental` join path.
4. Explain why recorded rentals are divided by distinct inventory copies.
5. Compare the worked investigation with at least two examples in the broader progression.
6. State why the result can prioritize an investigation but cannot prove unmet demand.
