# Chapter 1: Why PMs Need a Technical Floor

## Product question

> How close must I work to this product’s technical behavior, and where must specialist ownership remain explicit?

## Start with the visible artifacts

1. [Participation ladder figure](../figures/png/figure_1_1.png)
2. [Technical Floor Map](../templates/technical_floor_map.md)

No terminal or code is required.

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Name one user promise you are accountable for.
2. List the states, systems, data, integrations, and delivery steps behind it.
3. Mark your current participation level for this area: **ask**, **inspect**, **prototype**, or **contribute**.
4. Separate your decision rights from engineering, design, data, security, legal, and operational ownership.
5. Choose one action this week that follows the work one level further.

## Take it to your product

Complete one Technical Floor Map for a real product area. The useful output is not a technical-skills score; it is a specific participation boundary and practice plan tied to product behavior.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this chapter guide and `companion/templates/technical_floor_map.md`. Interview me about one product promise. Help me identify the systems and artifacts behind it, but do not assume access or expertise I have not confirmed. Distinguish what I can ask, inspect, prototype, or contribute from decisions that require a specialist. End with one bounded practice for the next seven days.

## Evidence boundary

This is a self-assessment and planning artifact, not a competency certification. It does not grant repository access, production authority, or specialist ownership.
