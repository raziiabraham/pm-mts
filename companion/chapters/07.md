> What decision will this experiment support, and what rule should exist before the result arrives?

## Five-minute walkthrough

1. Write the product decision and the mechanism you believe may change behavior.
2. Define hypothesis, unit, eligibility, assignment, comparison, and exposure.
3. Choose decision, guardrail, and diagnostic metrics before seeing results.
4. Record sample, duration, validity risks, and what would make the experiment inappropriate.
5. Write continue, revise, stop, or investigate rules before interpreting the supplied outputs.

## Take it to your product

Pre-register one experiment or explicitly record why experimentation is the wrong method. A qualitative test, operational pilot, or directional analysis may fit better when traffic, ethics, interference, or reversibility make an experiment weak.

## Ask an AI coding agent

> Read this guide, the interpretation lab, and the Experiment Card. Ask me for the decision before the hypothesis. Challenge the unit, eligibility, metrics, sample, and validity assumptions. Do not calculate a winning narrative from the supplied result until I have written a decision rule. Separate what the result estimates from what it cannot prove.

## Evidence boundary

Pre-registration reduces hindsight bias but does not make a weak design causal. Statistical significance, a positive metric, or an agent-generated interpretation cannot repair selection bias, interference, bad instrumentation, insufficient power, or an irrelevant decision metric.
